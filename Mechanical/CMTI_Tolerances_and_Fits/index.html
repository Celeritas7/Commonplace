ISO system of tolerances p.489–490

A toleranced dimension is written as a basic size followed by a tolerance zone made of two parts: a letter (the fundamental deviation — the position of the zone relative to the zero line) and a number (the IT grade — the width of the zone).

  • Fundamental deviation — lower-case letters a…zc for shafts, upper-case A…ZC for holes. Letters near the start (a, b, c…) sit far below the zero line (clearance); h/H touch the zero line; letters past k/K move into interference.
  • IT grade (IT01, IT0, IT1 … IT18) — the magnitude of the tolerance. Grades 01–4 for gauges, 5–11 for general engineering fits, 12–16 for rough/coarse tolerances.
Tolerance zone = letter (deviation) + grade (IT number) e.g. shaft “g6” = deviation g, grade IT6; hole “H7” = deviation H, grade IT7. Example: ⌀30 H7/g6.
Hole basis vs shaft basis

Hole basis (preferred): the hole is held at H and the fit is varied by changing the shaft letter. Shaft basis: the shaft is held at h and the hole letter is varied — used when one shaft size must carry several different mating parts (e.g. drawn bar stock). The handbook recommends ≈⌀20 IT9 for ABC bores and ⌀20 IT7 for others as a starting point.

Types of fits & recommended fits p.490–491

A fit is the relationship between a hole and shaft before assembly. There are three classes:

  • Clearance fit — shaft always smaller than hole (slide, close-running, running, easy-running, loose-running, extra-loose-running).
  • Transition fit — may give slight clearance or slight interference (push, wringing / easy-keying, light-keying, drive).
  • Interference (force) fit — shaft always larger than hole (light-press, press, heavy-press).

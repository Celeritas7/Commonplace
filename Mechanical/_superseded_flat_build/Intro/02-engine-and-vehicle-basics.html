Overview

A one-page quick-reference cluster covering three loosely related introductory topics: the three top-level engine parts, the fixed crankshaft-to-camshaft drive ratio, a one-word differential placeholder, and the active vs passive safety distinction. Each item here gets a deeper treatment elsewhere in the notebook — this page is a planted seed before the detail.

Working Principle

Engine parts (top-level)

The engine block is taught here as three big castings stacked vertically:

  1. Cylinder head — top casting; houses valves, cams, combustion chamber roof.
  2. Crank case — middle casting; houses crankshaft, conrods, pistons.
  3. Oil sump — bottom casting / pan; oil reservoir for lubrication and gravity drain-back.

Crankshaft–Camshaft ratio

For every 2 revolutions of the crankshaft, the camshaft turns once — a fixed 2 : 1 ratio in a 4-stroke engine. This is why intake/exhaust valves open once per 4 strokes (intake → compression → power → exhaust) rather than every revolution.

$$\frac{\text{Crankshaft rpm}}{\text{Camshaft rpm}} = \frac{2}{1}$$

Differential

Listed as a one-word star bullet — no expansion on this page. The differential is the gear train that lets the two driven wheels rotate at different speeds during a turn (outer wheel travels a longer arc than inner wheel).

Safety — Active vs Passive

Type Purpose Examples (filled in later in notebook)
Active safety Prevent an accident from happening ABS, DSC, brake assist, adaptive headlights
Passive safety Reduce impact / injury during an accident Airbags, crumple zones, seat belts, side-impact beams

Notebook wording: "Active — Prevent from accident; Passive — Reduces impact during accident."

Force Path / Process Flow

Crank → Cam drive (typical chain or belt)

Crankshaft (2 rev) ───► timing chain/belt ───► Camshaft (1 rev) ───► opens valves once/cycle
       │
       └── 2:1 reduction set by sprocket tooth count (cam sprocket = 2× crank sprocket teeth)

Components

Component Function
Cylinder head Houses valves, cams, spark plugs / injectors, combustion chamber.
Crank case Houses the crankshaft and supports the cylinder block where pistons reciprocate.
Oil sump Lower oil reservoir; gravity collects return oil from the engine.
Crankshaft Converts piston reciprocation → rotation; rotates at 2× camshaft speed.
Camshaft Opens/closes intake & exhaust valves; rotates at ½ crankshaft speed.
Differential Allows left and right driven wheels to turn at different rpm during a turn.

Visual Diagram

ASCII timing relationship:

4-stroke cycle:
Crank rev #1 ──►  Intake (valve open)    ──► Compression (valves closed)
Crank rev #2 ──►  Power   (valves closed) ──► Exhaust (valve open)

Cam rev #1 spans BOTH crank revs ─► one cam rotation = one full 4-stroke cycle = 2 crank rotations.

Key Facts

  • Three main engine castings: cylinder head, crank case, oil sump.
  • Crankshaft : Camshaft = 2 : 1 (4-stroke engines).
  • Differential = allows wheel-speed difference during turns.
  • Active safety prevents accidents; passive safety reduces injury during accidents.

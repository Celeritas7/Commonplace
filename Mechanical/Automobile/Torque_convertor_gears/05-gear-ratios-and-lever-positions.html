Torque Converter & Gears

Gear Ratios & Lever Positions

Source images: IMG20250514113419, IMG20250514113454

Overview

A summary of what gears are for, the 6-speed forward + reverse ratio chart for a typical BMW manual transmission, the final-drive (differential) ratio, and the selector lever position pattern that maps driver inputs to those gears. The numbers shown are representative of a 6-speed BMW manual gearbox of the E46/E60 era.

Working Principle

What gears are for (page header)

Notebook: "Transmission. Gear: ★ To provide diff. torque & at diff. power ★ Can take reverse — i.e. change of motion ★ To provide diff. speed ratios"

A transmission's three jobs:

# Job Why needed
1 Provide different torque at different power outputs An engine produces max torque in a narrow rpm band (peak around mid-rpm). Gears multiply that torque differently — high reduction in 1st (big wheel torque, low wheel speed), low reduction in top gear (low wheel torque, high wheel speed).
2 Take reverse Engines only rotate one direction. A reverse gear (with idler) reverses output direction so the car can back up.
3 Provide different speed ratios Lets the engine run efficiently across a wide range of road speeds — 1500 rpm at idle in 1st = 6 km/h; 1500 rpm in 6th = 120 km/h.

The gear ratios (notebook)

Notebook list (typical 6-speed BMW manual): ``` 1st gear: 2.97 : 1 2nd gear: 2.07 : 1 3rd gear: 1.43 : 1 4th gear: 1.00 : 1 ← direct drive (1:1) 5th gear: 0.84 : 1 ┐ 6th gear: 0.56 : 1 ┘ ← overdrive (≤ 1) Reverse: 3.28 : 1

Differential: 3.42 : 1 ```

Reading these: - 1st (2.97:1) — engine spins 2.97 times for each rotation of the gearbox output shaft → 2.97× torque multiplication, 1/2.97× speed. - 4th (1.00:1) — direct drive — gearbox output rpm = engine rpm. Most efficient (no gear-mesh losses inside the gearbox), used for medium-speed cruise. - 5th and 6th (< 1.0) — overdrive — gearbox output spins faster than engine → engine sits at lower rpm for the same road speed → better fuel economy at highway cruise. - Reverse (3.28:1) — usually similar to 1st gear ratio. Reverse uses a separate idler gear that reverses output direction. - Differential 3.42:1 — the final drive ratio at the rear axle. The propeller shaft spins 3.42 times for each rotation of the wheels. Total ratio = gearbox × diff.

Total reduction at the wheels

Real wheel torque depends on both the gearbox ratio and the differential ratio:

Gear Gearbox ratio × Diff (3.42) = Total reduction
1st 2.97 × 3.42 10.16 : 1 — huge multiplication, slow but very high wheel torque
2nd 2.07 × 3.42 7.08 : 1
3rd 1.43 × 3.42 4.89 : 1
4th 1.00 × 3.42 3.42 : 1
5th 0.84 × 3.42 2.87 : 1
6th 0.56 × 3.42 1.92 : 1 — minimum, low wheel torque, max road speed
Reverse 3.28 × 3.42 11.22 : 1

So at 5000 rpm in 6th, the wheels are spinning at 5000 / 1.92 = 2600 rpm — at a ~ 28-inch rolling diameter, that's about 170 km/h.

Force Path / Process Flow

   ENGINE (max torque around 4000 rpm)
        │
        ▼  output torque T_engine
   CLUTCH / TORQUE CONVERTER
        │
        ▼
   GEARBOX (multiplies torque by selected ratio R_gear)
        │
        ▼  torque = T_engine × R_gear
   PROPELLER SHAFT (RWD) or AXLE (FWD)
        │
        ▼
   DIFFERENTIAL (multiplies again by R_diff)
        │
        ▼  torque = T_engine × R_gear × R_diff
   WHEEL HUBS
        │
        ▼
   WHEEL TORQUE  →  road push  →  car accelerates

   ANGULAR SPEEDS go the other way:
        wheel rpm = engine rpm / (R_gear × R_diff)

Components

Component Function
Input shaft Where the clutch (or torque converter) feeds torque into the gearbox.
Lay shaft / counter shaft Parallel shaft (in manuals) carrying the constant-mesh gear pairs.
Output shaft Carries the chosen gear's output forward to the propeller shaft.
Gear pairs (1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 5th, 6th, reverse) Each ratio = one pair of gears in mesh.
Synchroniser rings Speed-match the input gear to the output shaft before the dog teeth engage — what makes the clutchless shift smooth in a manual.
Selector forks Move the synchros (and dog hubs) sideways to engage each gear.
Reverse idler Extra gear inserted into the reverse train to flip output rotation.
Differential Final reduction + lets left/right wheels turn at different speeds during a turn. See vehicle systems hierarchy → driveline.
Propeller shaft (RWD) Connects gearbox to rear axle.

Detailed Component Notes

Why 1st is so much higher than 6th

A car at rest needs the maximum wheel torque to overcome inertia + rolling resistance + any gradient. That's why 1st is 10:1 total or higher. At cruise the maximum road speed is wanted with low engine rpm for efficiency → 6th is barely 2:1 total.

Why the gap between gears narrows toward the top

Gap 1st→2nd 2nd→3rd 3rd→4th 4th→5th 5th→6th
Ratio drop 2.97→2.07 2.07→1.43 1.43→1.00 1.00→0.84 0.84→0.56
Factor 0.70× 0.69× 0.70× 0.84× 0.67×

Roughly geometric — each shift drops rpm by about 30% in the lower gears (so the engine stays in its torque band), narrower steps near direct drive. The 6th gear is a much bigger overdrive jump because it's specifically for highway fuel economy.

Why reverse ratio similar to 1st

Reverse uses similar gear sizes to 1st because the trade-off is the same: max torque to get a stationary car moving, accept low top speed (since you rarely reverse fast).

Differential 3.42:1 — typical BMW value

Common BMW final-drive ratios are 2.93, 3.07, 3.15, 3.46, 3.64, 3.91. 3.42 is a typical mid-range value for an inline-6 sedan. Sportier cars use 3.91 (more wheel torque, lower top speed); economy models use 2.93 (better cruise mpg, slower acceleration).

"Synchros" and the smooth shift

Not on this page, but worth knowing for completeness: when you push the lever from one gear to another in a manual, the synchroniser ring (a small brass cone clutch) speed-matches the upcoming gear to the output shaft before the dog teeth engage. This is what stops gears grinding — the driver doesn't have to double-declutch.

Key Facts

  • Gears do 3 jobs: provide different torque, allow reverse, give different speed ratios.
  • Typical BMW 6-speed ratios: 2.97 / 2.07 / 1.43 / 1.00 / 0.84 / 0.56, reverse 3.28, differential 3.42.
  • 4th gear = direct drive (1:1) — most efficient (no gear-mesh loss).
  • 5th and 6th gear < 1.0 = overdrive — output spins faster than input → low engine rpm at highway speed → fuel economy.
  • Total reduction = gearbox ratio × differential ratio. 1st gear total = 10.16:1; 6th gear total = 1.92:1.
  • Reverse uses an idler gear to flip output direction; ratio similar to 1st.
  • Final drive (differential) ratio is a fixed value of ~3:1 to 4:1, picked for the car's performance vs economy intent.
  • Selector pattern is H-pattern; reverse usually has a lockout (collar lift, push down) to prevent accidental engagement.
  • Synchros speed-match gears for smooth shifts (not in notebook but critical to manual operation).

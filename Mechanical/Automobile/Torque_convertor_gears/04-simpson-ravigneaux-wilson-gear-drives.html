Torque Converter & Gears

Simpson, Ravigneaux & Wilson Compound Gear Drives

Source images: IMG20250514181348, IMG20250514181405

Overview

A single planetary gear set gives at most a handful of ratios (see epicyclic planetary gears). To get the 3, 4, 5, 6 forward speeds + reverse that an automatic transmission needs, multiple planetary sets are combined into a compound gear train. The page covers the three classic compound layouts: Simpson (two sets sharing a sun), Ravigneaux (one set with two sun gears + two planet sizes), and Wilson (three sets in series). The dealer-trainer used Simpson as the worked example for first/second/third/reverse gear selection.

Working Principle

Why combine planetary sets

A single set gives 6 modes (= 6 ratios), but adjacent ratios in those 6 are often too far apart for practical driving — you'd jump from 3:1 to 1:1 with nothing useful in between. Combining sets lets you stack ratios → you can get progressively-spaced gears: e.g., 3.21 → 1.94 → 1.32 → 1.00 → 0.75 (a typical 5-speed AT spread).

Simpson planetary gear drive

Notebook: "★ Simpson planetary gear drive – sun gear — same – The planetary gear carrier of 2nd epicyclic gear train is attached to ring of 1st epicyclic gear train."

Architecture: two planetary sets share a common sun gear, and the carrier of set 2 is connected to the ring gear of set 1. So you have: - Sun gear (shared between both sets) - Set 1: sun (shared) + planets + carrier (independent) + ring (linked to set 2 carrier) - Set 2: sun (shared) + planets + carrier (linked to set 1 ring) + ring (independent)

This gives enough free elements (independent input/output choices + multi-plate brakes/clutches) to select 3 forward gears + reverse with reasonable spread.

Simpson gear selection (notebook walkthrough)

Notebook (lower half of page):

"1st gear: (1) … (content abbreviated on page)

2nd gear: ② Sun gears held stationary

3rd gear: ③ Sun & planetary gear carrier held

Reverse: ① held 1st planetary gear's carrier

Then a 2nd version:

1st gear: i) The ring gear of 1st epicyclic gear train is held ii) I/P is given to sun gear

2nd gear: i) Sun gears held stationary

3rd gear: i) Sun & planetary gear (carrier) held [together = 1:1 direct drive]

Reverse: i) held 1st planetary gear's carrier"

In Simpson terms:

Gear What is held What is input Result
1st Ring of set 1 Sun (input shaft) Big reduction (compound effect of both sets)
2nd Both sun gears (effectively) Sun and carrier driven together Mild reduction
3rd Sun and planet carrier locked together Whole assembly rotates as one 1:1 direct drive
Reverse Planet carrier of set 1 Sun Reverse (carrier-held planetary inverts direction — see mode 5/6 from epicyclic chapter)

The Simpson is the workhorse of older 3-speed and 4-speed American automatics (e.g., Chrysler TorqueFlite, GM Turbo-Hydramatic).

Ravigneaux planetary gear drive

Notebook (IMG 181405 top): "1st: Planetary gear carrier having: ① large planetary gear partially connected to ring gear of 2nd one. ② Planetary gears of 2nd have large dia than long planetary gears, & connected to ring gear of 2nd one.

2nd: Small sun fixed.

3rd: Any two of [three held].

4th: Small sun fixed, Big sun gear rotating.

Reverse: Planetary gear carrier held."

A Ravigneaux set is more compact than two Simpson sets — it puts everything on one carrier with two sun gears (small and big) and two sizes of planet gears (short and long):

Element Description
Small sun gear Meshes with the short planets
Big sun gear Meshes with the long planets
Long planets Mesh with big sun AND with short planets
Short planets Mesh with small sun AND with long planets AND with ring gear
Ring gear Surrounds everything, meshes with short planets
Carrier Carries all planets (short + long)

The ratios come from picking which sun is input, which is held, and whether the carrier or ring is output.

Ravigneaux gear selection (notebook)

Gear Selection
1st Planetary gear carrier configured with large planets connected partially to ring gear of set 2 (compound action — high reduction)
2nd Small sun fixed (small-sun-held mode)
3rd Any two of three held → 1:1 direct drive
4th Small sun fixed + big sun rotating → mild overdrive
Reverse Planet carrier held → reverse

The Ravigneaux is more compact than Simpson and is the standard in rear-wheel-drive 4-speed automatics (e.g., BMW ZF 4HP18, 4HP22, 4HP24 — all in the dealer-training era).

Wilson type gear unit

Notebook (IMG 181405 bottom): "Wilson type gear unit: – 3 sets of planetary gears – [continues but cut off]"

A Wilson gearbox uses three or more planetary sets in series, each individually controllable via brake bands. Pre-selector mechanism (driver selects next gear, system shifts on demand). Historically used in: - Pre-WW2 British cars (Daimler, Armstrong Siddeley, ENV). - Heavy military vehicles (tanks). - Modern application: the ZF 8HP 8-speed automatic in current BMWs is conceptually descended from this multi-set approach (uses 4 planetary sets + multiple shift elements).

The "3 sets of planetary gears" gives many more selectable ratios than Simpson (2 sets) or Ravigneaux (compound 1-set equivalent).

Force Path / Process Flow

Simpson — gear-by-gear shift logic

   1ST GEAR (high reduction):
   ──────────────────────────
   Engine ─► Sun gear (driven)
                 │
                 ▼
            Both sets work in series; ring of set 1 held → big reduction
                 │
                 ▼
          Output = carrier of set 2

   2ND GEAR (mild reduction):
   ──────────────────────────
   Engine ─► Sun gear
                 │
   Ring of set 1 NOW FREE; carrier of set 2 partly held → moderate reduction
                 │
                 ▼
          Output = carrier of set 2

   3RD GEAR (1:1 direct drive):
   ────────────────────────────
   Engine ─► Sun + ring of set 1 LOCKED TOGETHER (clutch)
                 │
            → whole compound set rotates as one
                 │
                 ▼
          Output = same as input speed

   REVERSE:
   ────────
   Engine ─► Sun gear
                 │
                 ▼
   Carrier of set 1 HELD (multi-plate brake)
                 │
                 ▼
   Planets become idlers → ring of set 1 spins in reverse
                 │
                 ▼
          Output = REVERSE direction

Ravigneaux — gear-by-gear shift logic

   1ST GEAR: small sun in, ring out, big sun held → high reduction
   2ND GEAR: small sun in, ring out, carrier held → moderate reduction
   3RD GEAR: lock any two of three elements together → 1:1 direct
   4TH GEAR: small sun held, big sun in, ring out → mild overdrive
   REVERSE: small sun in, carrier held → reverse direction

   ► All ratios from ONE compact compound set.

Components

Component Function
Sun gear (Simpson — shared) Single central gear common to both planetary sets.
Small + big sun gears (Ravigneaux) Two coaxial sun gears, each meshes with different planet sets.
Long + short planets (Ravigneaux) Two different planet sizes — long planets bridge big sun to ring; short planets mesh small sun to long planets.
Planet carrier (1 in Ravigneaux, 2 in Simpson) Carries the planet gears in orbit.
Ring gears (1 in Ravigneaux, 2 in Simpson) Outer gears with internal teeth.
Multi-plate brakes (one per held element) Hydraulically apply to ground a planetary element to the case — see multi-plate brake/clutch.
Multi-plate clutches (for "any-two-locked" direct drive) Lock two rotating elements together for 1:1 gear.
One-way clutches (some applications) Hold an element on coast but freewheel on overrun — used for smooth 1-2 shift.
Valve body Hydraulic logic that decides which brake/clutch is applied for each commanded gear.
EGS / TCU The electronic control unit that commands the valve body — see EGS chapter.

Detailed Component Notes

Simpson vs Ravigneaux — when which

Criterion Simpson Ravigneaux
Number of planetary sets 2 (in series) 1 (compound)
Axial length Longer Shorter
Number of forward ratios 3 (4-speed needs an aux ratio) 4
Manufacturing Two standard sets Custom two-sun/two-planet-size set
Historic use American 3-speed automatics (Powerglide, TorqueFlite, TH350) European 4-speed RWD (BMW ZF, Mercedes 4G-Tronic)

BMW's classic 4-speed ZF transmissions of the dealer-training era (ZF 4HP18/22/24) are Ravigneaux-based.

Wilson and the modern multi-speed automatic

The modern ZF 8HP 8-speed (used in every BMW since 2009) uses four planetary sets with five shift elements (multi-plate clutches and brakes). That's even more elements than the classic Wilson — descended from the same multi-set principle. The page's "3 sets of planetary gears" Wilson reference is the historical seed of this modern multi-speed approach.

Why the "any two of three → 1:1" trick is so heavily used

Cross-ref: epicyclic chapter — 1:1 rule

Direct drive is the most efficient gear (no gear-mesh losses). On a 4-speed auto, 3rd or 4th is direct drive — the gear most cars cruise in on highway. Designed deliberately to maximise efficiency on the longest-duration operating regime.

Key Facts

  • Compound planetary = multiple planetary sets linked → more ratios.
  • Simpson = 2 sets, shared sun, carrier of set 2 linked to ring of set 1. Gives 3 forward + reverse.
  • Ravigneaux = 1 compound set with 2 suns + 2 planet sizes + 1 carrier + 1 ring. Gives 4 forward + reverse in a more compact package.
  • Wilson = 3+ planetary sets in series. Historical pre-selector gearbox. Conceptual ancestor of modern 8/9/10-speed automatics.
  • Simpson 1st gear: ring of set 1 held, sun input → high reduction.
  • Simpson 3rd gear (direct): sun + ring of set 1 locked → 1:1.
  • Simpson reverse: carrier of set 1 held → planets become idlers → output reverses direction.
  • Ravigneaux 3rd gear (direct): any two of three elements locked → 1:1.
  • BMW ZF 4HP series (dealer-training era) are Ravigneaux-based 4-speed RWD automatics.
  • Modern ZF 8HP in current BMWs uses 4 planetary sets — direct descendant of the Wilson principle.

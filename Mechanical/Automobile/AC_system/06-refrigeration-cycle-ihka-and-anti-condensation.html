Overview

Two pages that close the AC module. The first is dated 25/7/2013 — a new training day on which the trainer re-explains the full refrigeration cycle from scratch with cleaner annotations, naming the pressure/temperature state at each leg, listing the function of every component, and stating two summary facts the previous treatment didn't put in one place: A/C is a heat pump, and the high-pressure line runs 12–20 bar while the low-pressure line runs at low single-digit bar. The second page (003751) introduces the IHKA — Integrated Heating-Klimatisierung-Automatik — the BMW control module that bundles heating, ventilation, and air-conditioning into one closed-loop automatic system. A/C is a heat pump

"A/C is heat pump." — IMG 002215.

A heat pump is any device that moves heat up a temperature gradient — from a cooler reservoir to a hotter one — by consuming work input. Refrigerators, freezers, and air-conditioners are all heat pumps; they differ only in which reservoir the user cares about:

Device Cool reservoir (heat absorbed from) Hot reservoir (heat rejected to) What the user wants
Refrigerator Food compartment Kitchen air Food stays cold
Freezer Freezer compartment Kitchen air Food stays frozen
Car A/C Cabin air Outside ambient air Cabin stays cool
Building heat pump (winter) Outside air Building interior Interior stays warm

The trainer's point: even though we call it "air conditioning," the underlying machine is the exact same kind of thermodynamic device as a kitchen fridge — just plumbed differently and oriented around the user's interest in cabin temperature rather than food temperature.

Function of expansion valve — summary

"Function of expansion valve: ★ Controls the flow of refrigerant in the evaporator." — IMG 002215.

The TXV's mechanical-feedback operation (file 04) yields one outcome: how much refrigerant per unit time enters the evaporator. Too much → flooding → evaporator over-cools → icing risk. Too little → starvation → evaporator under-cools → poor cooling capacity. The TXV's self-regulating sensing-bulb design holds the flow rate at the sweet spot continuously.

IHKA — Integrated Heating-Klimatisierung-Automatik

The second page introduces BMW's combined HVAC control system. IHKA is the German abbreviation: Integrated Heating-Klimatisierung (Klimatisierung = air conditioning) Automatik (Automatik = automatic). It is one ECU that handles heating, ventilation, and air conditioning as a single closed-loop automatic system, reading sensors and commanding actuators.

Three inputs: - Condensation sensor — detects humidity at the windshield/cabin; triggers anti-misting countermeasures. - Exterior temp sensor — ambient air temperature outside. - Interior temp sensor — cabin air temperature.

Five output actuators: - Compressor — engages/disengages or modulates displacement. - Flap motor — drives the temperature mixing flap (blend door) and other ducting flaps. - Blower — sets cabin fan speed. - Evaporator — receives the temperature sensor signal (and indirectly modulates compressor). - Heat exchanger — the heater core valve (on water-side systems) or its airflow path (on air-side systems).

Anti-condensation countermeasures

"Counter measures: — by component of IHKA — condensation sensor shuts off the AC." — IMG 003751.

Window condensation (windscreen, side glass) appears when humid cabin air contacts cold glass and dew-point is reached. This causes immediate safety risk (poor visibility). The IHKA's condensation sensor detects rising humidity and triggers a four-step response before misting becomes visible:

"Measures of integrated automatic heating & AC system: ① Increase blower air flow. ② Opens defrost flaps wider. ③ Increase inside temp. ④ Disables recirculating air mode & adds fresh air." — IMG 003751.

# Action Why it helps
① Increase blower air flow More airflow across the windscreen sweeps away condensation as it tries to form
② Open defrost flaps wider Routes more airflow to the windshield base (the defroster outlets) where condensation usually starts
③ Increase inside temperature Warmer air holds more moisture in vapour form before reaching dew-point; cabin glass warms up too
④ Disable recirculating mode, add fresh air Outside air on a cold day is usually drier than cabin air; bringing it in dilutes cabin humidity

The sequence is escalating — if step ① doesn't clear the condensation within a few seconds, the system adds step ②, then ③, then ④. Step ④ is the most aggressive (fuel-economy penalty from cooling all that fresh outside air) and is reserved for severe misting.

Step zero — when the condensation sensor first detects rising humidity — is "the condensation sensor shuts off the AC." This sounds counter-intuitive (why turn off the cooling system when there's misting?), but the logic is: if the cabin is already cold and the windows are misting from outside, the AC dehumidification isn't the answer — adding heat is. So the system steps the cooling down while ramping the heater up.

Components

Component Function
IHKA control unit Central HVAC ECU — reads 3 sensors, commands 5 actuators

Exterior temperature sensor Reads outside ambient (usually mounted behind the front bumper)
Interior temperature sensor Reads cabin air (often inside the headliner or dashboard)
Flap motor (servo) Positions the blend door, defrost flaps, recirculation flap
Cabin blower fan Variable-speed motor for HVAC airflow

Key Facts

  • A/C is a heat pump — the same thermodynamic device as a kitchen refrigerator, plumbed for cabin cooling.
  • High-pressure line: 12–20 bar. Low-pressure line: low single-digit bar.
  • The TXV controls the flow of refrigerant into the evaporator — self-regulating via its sensing bulb (file 04).
  • IHKA = Integrated Heating-Klimatisierung-Automatik — BMW's combined HVAC controller.
  • IHKA reads 3 sensors (condensation, exterior temp, interior temp) and drives 5 actuators (compressor, flap motor, blower, evaporator, heat exchanger).
  • Anti-condensation countermeasure is a 4-step escalation: ① blower up, ② defrost flap open, ③ heater up, ④ recirc off + fresh air in.
  • When condensation is first detected, the AC compressor is briefly shut off so the heater can dominate — counter-intuitive but correct.

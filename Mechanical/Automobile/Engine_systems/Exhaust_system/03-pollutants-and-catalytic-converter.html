Pollutants & Catalytic Converter

Source images: IMG20250514112321

Overview

The three regulated automotive pollutants вҖ” CO, HC, NOx вҖ” and what the catalytic converter does to them. The page contrasts the pellet-type and monolithic (honeycomb) catalyst constructions and the two-way vs three-way converter difference. It also touches on the muffler's role (back-pressure reduction, Pt/Pd/Rh material), lead in petrol (lubricating property, now phased out), and diesel film formation.

Working Principle

The combustion equation (ideal vs real)

Notebook: "Fuel + Air вҶ’ HC + OвӮӮ + N вҶ’ HвӮӮO + COвӮӮ + N"

Ideal combustion of a hydrocarbon fuel: $$\text{C}_x\text{H}_y + \text{O}_2 + \text{N}_2 \;\longrightarrow\; \text{CO}_2 + \text{H}_2\text{O} + \text{N}_2$$

This would produce nothing but COвӮӮ, water, and inert nitrogen. In reality, combustion is incomplete and high-temperature, so you also get:

Pollutant Source What it is
CO (carbon monoxide) Incomplete combustion (not enough OвӮӮ) Toxic; bonds to haemoglobin.
HC (unburnt hydrocarbons) Misfire, cold engine, rich mixture Smog precursor.
NOx (nitrogen oxides) High combustion temperature вҶ’ NвӮӮ + OвӮӮ react Acid rain + smog.

Notebook: "HC & CO level decreased by modern system" вҖ” meaning closed-loop EFI + cat dropped HC and CO by an order of magnitude over carb-era cars. NOx is the harder one.

Lead in petrol (historical note)

Notebook: "Lead (Pb) is added to impart lubricating property."

Tetra-ethyl lead (TEL) was added to petrol from the 1920s to about 2000: - Boosted octane (anti-knock) cheaply. - Lubricated valve seats in soft-iron heads вҖ” the lead deposits acted as a sacrificial cushion.

Phased out globally because: - Poisons catalytic converters (the lead coats the Pt/Pd/Rh and kills activity). - Toxic to humans and ecosystems.

Modern petrol uses oxygenates (MTBE, ethanol) for octane and modern alloy heads have hardened valve seats that don't need lead lubrication.

Diesel film

Notebook: "Diesel can form film."

Diesel fuel has higher viscosity and oilier character than petrol вҖ” it can form a thin lubricant film on cylinder walls, useful in some applications (e.g., HPDI fuel injectors in older diesels self-lubricate from the diesel itself). Why it's listed here on the pollutants page is unclear вҖ” possibly a sidebar comparing fuels.

Muffler / silencer

Notebook: "Muffler: 1. Back pressure is reduced in muffler. 2. Pt, Palladium, Rhodium."

Two points conflated on one bullet: 1. Muffler design вҖ” internal baffles and Helmholtz chambers cancel sound waves while keeping flow restriction (back-pressure) as low as possible. High back-pressure = engine has to push harder against exhaust = power loss. Modern mufflers balance noise vs flow. 2. Pt/Pd/Rh вҖ” these are the catalytic converter precious-metal coatings, not muffler materials. Almost certainly a notebook layout error: the trainer wrote the catalyst metals on the muffler bullet. Kept in original order; corrected interpretation in the Components table.

Catalytic converter вҖ” pellet vs monolithic

Type Construction Notes
Pellet type Contains a bed made up of thousands of small beads coated with the catalyst. Exhaust gas passes over the beads. Older design; vibration / heat-shock breaks beads вҶ’ catalyst migration & restriction. Largely obsolete.
Monolithic type A honeycomb ceramic block (cordierite) with thin walls coated with Pt, Pd, & Rh mounted in a stainless-steel container. Modern standard. Lower back-pressure than pellet; better heat shock resistance; lower precious-metal loading per cat.

Two-way vs three-way converter

Type Reduces / Oxidises Catalyst Fitment
Two-way (oxidation cat) HC + CO вҶ’ COвӮӮ + HвӮӮO Pt, Pd Older petrol cars (pre-1989), modern diesel engines (NOx handled separately).
Three-way HC + CO + NOx вҶ’ COвӮӮ + HвӮӮO + NвӮӮ Pt + Pd + Rh (Rh does NOx reduction) All modern petrol cars. Requires О» = 1 closed-loop operation.

Notebook: "Two-way catalytic converter вҖ” HC & CO" / "Three-way converter вҖ” [also NOx via Rh]"

The rhodium is what enables NOx reduction at the same time as HC/CO oxidation; it's also the most expensive of the three (ВЈ10,000+/kg historically) вҖ” which is why used cats are stripped for precious metals.

Components

Detailed Component Notes

Why honeycomb beats pellet

  • Lower back-pressure вҖ” straight channels through the honeycomb vs tortuous path through beads.
  • Faster light-off вҖ” thin walls heat up quickly when the cold engine first starts (catalyst is inactive below ~250 В°C).
  • Mechanical robustness вҖ” honeycomb survives engine vibration better; beads pulverise over time.

Why a "three-way" cat needs О» = 1

  • The oxidation reaction (HC, CO вҶ’ COвӮӮ + HвӮӮO) wants excess OвӮӮ вҶ’ lean exhaust.
  • The reduction reaction (NOx вҶ’ NвӮӮ) wants excess reducing agent (CO and HвӮӮ) вҶ’ rich exhaust.
  • The two needs are opposite. Only at О» = 1 (exactly stoichiometric) does the exhaust carry just enough OвӮӮ for oxidation while leaving just enough CO for reduction. Both reactions proceed at high rate in the narrow stoichiometric window.

Lead poisoning the cat

A leaded-fuel tank into a cat-equipped car will kill the converter in a few hundred kilometres вҖ” Pb deposits coat the precious metal sites. This is why introduction of unleaded fuel was a hard prerequisite for the rollout of 3-way cats in the 1970sвҖ“80s.

What about diesel particulate filter (DPF)?

Not in the notes (post-dates 2007 mandates). A DPF is a wall-flow filter that traps soot particles, then regenerates by burning them off periodically. Diesel after-treatment in the modern era is DOC (diesel oxidation cat, 2-way) вҶ’ DPF вҶ’ SCR / NOx storage for NOx.

Key Facts

  • 3 regulated pollutants: CO, HC, NOx. Plus COвӮӮ (greenhouse, not toxic but regulated separately).
  • Ideal combustion: Fuel + air вҶ’ COвӮӮ + HвӮӮO + NвӮӮ. Real combustion adds CO, HC, NOx.
  • Catalyst metals: Pt + Pd (oxidation), Rh (reduction).
  • Two-way cat = HC + CO only (diesel). Three-way cat = also NOx (petrol at О» = 1).
  • Pellet vs monolithic вҖ” modern cats are monolithic honeycomb (lower back-pressure, faster light-off, more robust).
  • Lead in petrol lubricated valves AND boosted octane historically вҖ” but poisons cats, so phased out.
  • Muffler reduces back-pressure while cancelling noise (the trainer accidentally tagged the cat metals onto the muffler bullet).
  • Catalyst light-off temperature вүҲ 250вҖ“300 В°C; cold-start emissions dominate total trip emissions because of this lag.

Overview

A complete top-down breakdown of every major system on the vehicle, used as the master map for the rest of the notebook. The trainer organised the entire car under 14 numbered systems, with the engine itself further decomposed into 7 sub-systems and the driveline into 3. Every later notebook section sits somewhere on this tree.

Working Principle

Treat this page as the index of the vehicle: any later detail (cooling, brakes, airbags, MoDic, navigation, etc.) maps back to one node here. The hierarchy mirrors the way BMW E60/E65-era service literature segments the car.

Components

# System What it contains / does
1 Engine Converts fuel energy → rotational mechanical energy. Sub-systems: air induction, fuel, ignition, exhaust, cooling, lubrication, engine electrical. Hardware: cylinder head, crank case, oil sump and all internals.
2 Transmission Gears — selects torque/speed ratios between engine and driveline.
3 Drive line Takes torque from transmission to wheels: propeller shaft → differential → axle shaft. Routes power to front, rear, or all wheels.
4 Wheels & tyre Road interface — supports the vehicle and transmits longitudinal/lateral forces.
5 Brake system Decelerates the vehicle, holds it stationary.
6 Steering system Translates driver input into wheel-angle change.
7 Chassis & suspension system Frame plus springs/dampers/links — supports body, isolates road inputs, locates wheels.
8 Safety system Active (prevent crash) and passive (reduce injury).
9 Vehicle electrical system 12 V power distribution, lighting, body electrics, harnessing.
10 Engine management system Sensors → ECU/DME → actuators control loop for engine.
11 Air condition system Cabin heating, cooling, dehumidification.
12 Navigation system Map + GPS + route guidance.
13 Body shell Outer/inner sheet-metal structure, crash structure.
14 Infotainment system Audio, video, connectivity, head unit.

Detailed Component Notes

Why split "Vehicle electrical" from "Engine management"

Vehicle electrical (#9) is the bus and bulb side — body wiring, lights, comfort modules. Engine management (#10) is the closed-loop control system for the engine specifically (DME on BMW). They share the same physical wiring but are separate functional domains in dealer training.

Why split "Cylinder head / Crank case / Oil sump" from the 7 sub-systems

The first three are physical castings; the 7 sub-systems (a–g) are functional systems that thread through those castings. A single component like a head gasket lives in the cylinder-head casting but is part of the cooling system.

Key Facts

  • 14 top-level vehicle systems organise the rest of the notebook.
  • Engine has 7 functional sub-systems (a–g) and 3 main castings (cylinder head / crank case / oil sump).
  • Driveline = propeller shaft + differential + axle shaft, feeding front and/or rear wheels.
  • Engine management = sensors → control unit → actuators (BMW DME).
  • Safety splits into active (prevention) and passive (mitigation).

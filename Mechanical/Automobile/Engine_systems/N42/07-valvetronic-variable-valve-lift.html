N42 Engine

Valvetronic — Fully Variable Valve Lift

Source images: IMG20250515003000, IMG20250515003012

Overview

Valvetronic is BMW's fully-variable valve-lift control system — the lift of the intake valve is varied continuously from about 0.3 mm to ~10 mm by an electric motor turning an eccentric shaft, which moves an intermediate lever between the cam and the valve. This replaces the throttle as the engine's load-control device, eliminating most of the pumping losses that hurt part-load fuel economy on conventional petrol engines. N42 was the first BMW production engine to ship it (2001).

Working Principle

Three core components

Notebook: 1. Valves & roller-type finger [follower] 2. Intermediate lever 3. Eccentric shaft

The mechanical chain from cam to valve becomes:

   Camshaft ──► Intermediate lever ──► Roller finger follower ──► Valve
                       ▲
                       │
              Eccentric shaft (turned by motor under DME control)
              changes the geometry of the intermediate lever
              → varies the effective lift transmitted to the valve

When the eccentric shaft rotates, it shifts the fulcrum of the intermediate lever. Tiny eccentric-shaft rotation → big change in transmitted valve lift. Lift sweeps continuously from 0.3 mm (essentially closed — minimum air mass for idle) to ~10 mm (full lift — wide-open-throttle equivalent).

Why this kills throttle losses

  • Conventional petrol engine: load controlled by closing the throttle → manifold vacuum → piston works against vacuum on intake stroke → pumping loss.
  • N42 with Valvetronic: throttle stays wide open → manifold near atmospheric → no pumping loss. Air mass into cylinder is set by how much the intake valve opens (and for how long) → meters air at the valve, not at the throttle.

Result: roughly 10–15% better fuel economy at part-load (city, cruise) — the dominant operating regime.

Advantages

Notebook: "Adv:- i) More power & torque ii) Better ride quality iii) More spontaneous change in driving condition iv) Greater dynamic range"

Advantage Why
More power & torque At full load, Valvetronic can hold valve open longer + higher lift than a fixed cam allows → better cylinder filling.
Better ride quality Throttle response is smoother because lift varies continuously, not stepped through a throttle plate.
More spontaneous response Eccentric-shaft motor responds in ~ 300 ms; throttle response feels immediate because air flow tracks driver pedal directly.
Greater dynamic range Lift swings from 0.3 mm to ~10 mm — a factor of ~33×. No throttle plate can match that air-flow dynamic range.

Valvetronic control loop

Motor (12 V max, 40 A max). - Eccentric shaft (fully open). - "Locking to maximum lift condition" arrow points at variable-valve-lift. - Numbered flow between Variable Valve Lift, Sensor, Valvetronic, DME, Throttle valve: 1. Error recognised (sensor) 2. Message sent to it (to Valvetronic from sensor) 3. Closed (Valvetronic → variable valve lift) 4. Error msg sent to it (to DME) 5. Order to solve problem on own (DME → throttle valve)

Reading this as a closed-loop with fault tolerance built in:

Step What happens
1 Position sensor on eccentric shaft detects an error (e.g., motor stuck, shaft over-current).
2 Sensor reports the error to the Valvetronic control unit.
3 Valvetronic control unit closes / disengages the variable valve lift (locks to a safe lift).
4 Valvetronic control unit reports the error message to the DME.
5 DME hands load control over to the throttle valve (limp-home mode — see features and air routing for the throttle's emergency role).

"Locking to maximum lift condition"

If the system fails open or loses position reference, the eccentric shaft physically defaults to maximum lift — same as a conventional engine running wide-open valves. The throttle then takes over load control. The car drives, you get a check-engine light, but you don't get stranded.

Reprogramming after replacement

Notebook ✩: "Reprogramming of valvetronic control unit is necessary (otherwise ECU will not accept it)."

A new Valvetronic ECU has to be coded to the car (zero-position of the eccentric shaft + paired calibration with DME). Swap-and-drive doesn't work — you need a BMW-specific scan tool (DIS / ISTA) to write the calibration. See diagnosis chapter.

Throttle-valve behaviour by ambient temperature

Notebook (IMG 003012): "When ambient temperature is: 1. Cold: Throttle valve fully open & more amount of air enter to reduce warm-up period. 2. Hot: Throttle valve is MAP-controlled which allow entrance of certain amount of air in cylinder & there is ignition." (Margin: "Acts as choke")

This is the residual job of the throttle on a Valvetronic engine: - Cold start — throttle fully open. Maximum air flow into the engine → reduces warm-up time (more combustion heat per second). - Hot operation — throttle position is MAP-controlled (modulated to maintain the right manifold pressure for whatever auxiliary systems need vacuum — see throttle's 5 roles). Acts a bit like a choke in regulating cylinder filling alongside Valvetronic.

Components

Component Function
Camshaft Standard cam with fixed lobe profile — does not vary.
Intermediate lever The clever bit — pivot point is moved by the eccentric shaft to vary valve lift transmitted to the follower.
Eccentric shaft Rotated by the Valvetronic motor; its rotation moves the intermediate-lever pivot.
Roller-type finger follower Low-friction follower between intermediate lever and valve stem.
Valvetronic motor 12 V brushed DC, up to 40 A peak. Drives the eccentric shaft.
Eccentric shaft position sensor Closed-loop position feedback to Valvetronic control unit.
Valvetronic control unit Dedicated ECU (separate from DME) that drives the motor and reports status to DME.
DME Master ECU; commands a target lift to the Valvetronic unit; takes over load control if Valvetronic fails.
Throttle valve Limp-home fallback; otherwise nearly fully open (see features chapter).
Springs (in valvetrain) Standard valve springs return valves; Valvetronic uses slightly stiffer springs because of the higher lift dynamics.

Detailed Component Notes

Why "not present in modern N52"

Notebook margin tag: "Not present in modern N52" (struck through then re-written)

This is incorrect as written — N52 also has Valvetronic (N52 was the inline-six successor to N42's V-tech and definitely uses Valvetronic). The trainer's struck-through note might be referring to a specific aftermarket comparison, or it might be a slip. See Open Questions.

What is true: the N42 used the first-generation Valvetronic; later engines (N52, N53, N54, N55) progressively refined it with smaller motors, faster response, and lower friction.

The 0.3 mm minimum lift

At 0.3 mm of lift, the intake valve is barely cracked — restrictive enough that only a few hundred mg of air enters the cylinder per cycle. That's enough for idle (~700 rpm) but no more. The DME can hold lift right at the idle target rather than throttling air with a closed butterfly.

Why the motor needs 40 A

Sweeping the eccentric shaft against valve-spring pressure requires significant torque. The motor isn't continuously at 40 A — it's a peak draw during fast lift changes (sudden pedal jab, downshift). At steady cruise the motor barely draws anything (eccentric shaft stays put).

The hot/cold throttle behaviour as "choke"

The trainer's "acts as choke" margin tag is a useful framing — on a cold engine the throttle stays open to maximise warm-up airflow (carburetor-era choke also enriched the mixture, but here the analogy is air not fuel). On a hot engine the throttle modulates to manage manifold pressure for accessory systems.

Key Facts

  • Valvetronic = continuously variable valve lift (not timing — that's VANOS).
  • Lift range: 0.3 mm (idle) to ~ 10 mm (full power) — about 33× dynamic range.
  • Mechanism: eccentric shaft turns under DME-controlled motor → moves intermediate-lever pivot → changes lift transmitted by roller finger to valve.
  • Replaces the throttle as primary load-control device → eliminates pumping losses → ~10–15% fuel-economy gain at part load.
  • Motor: 12 V, 40 A peak.
  • N42 first BMW production engine with Valvetronic (2001).
  • Failure mode: locks to maximum lift; throttle valve takes over as limp-home load control.
  • Valvetronic control unit must be coded after replacement (BMW DIS / ISTA).
  • Throttle valve roles when Valvetronic active:
  • Cold — fully open (max warm-up airflow).
  • Hot — MAP-controlled (modulates for vacuum auxiliaries, acts like choke).
  • 4 advantages: more power/torque, better ride quality, more spontaneous response, greater dynamic range.

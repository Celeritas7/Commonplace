Cylinder Head — Camshaft Layouts (OHV / OHC / SOHC / DOHC)

Source images: IMG20250514111924

Overview

A taxonomy of how the camshaft can be arranged relative to the cylinder head, with the four standard layouts (OHV, OHC, SOHC, DOHC) and one line of pros/cons each. This is the basis for understanding why modern BMW engines (N42/N52/N62) use DOHC with variable timing (VANOS) rather than older pushrod (OHV) designs.

Working Principle

The job of the camshaft is to open the intake and exhaust valves at the right crank angle. Where you put the camshaft — down in the block vs on top of the head — and how many you use changes (a) how much valvetrain mass moves at high rpm, (b) how easy it is to add multiple valves per cylinder, and (c) whether you can independently time intakes and exhausts.

Detailed Component Notes

Why "OHV" is sometimes called "Pushrod"

In OHV, the valves are still on top of the head (hence the name), but the cam is in the block, so it needs a long pushrod going up to a rocker arm to open the valve. The reciprocating mass of the pushrod limits safe rpm.

Why DOHC enables 4 valves per cylinder

With two cams sitting parallel above the head, you can stand the intake and exhaust valves at a wide included angle and fit 4 (or 5) valves per cylinder cleanly. A SOHC head with one cam doing both jobs is hard to lay out for more than 2 valves per cylinder without a complex finger-follower system.

Why DOHC enables variable timing

Because intake and exhaust each have their own camshaft, you can phase them independently — the basis of BMW's double-VANOS system on N42/N52 (continuously variable timing on both intake and exhaust cams).

Key Facts

  • OHV = cam in block, pushrod-driven valves.
  • OHC = cam(s) on head; covers SOHC and DOHC.
  • SOHC = 1 camshaft per head, operates intake + exhaust together.
  • DOHC = 2 camshafts per head, separate intake & exhaust cams.
  • DOHC pros: efficiency, multi-valve, variable timing.
  • DOHC cons: complexity, cost.
  • BMW N42/N52/N62 (this notebook's focus engines) are all DOHC with VANOS.

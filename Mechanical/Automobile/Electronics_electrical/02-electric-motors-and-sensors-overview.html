Working Principle

Electric motor — basic anatomy

Notebook (IMG 113404): "Electric motors: Stator, rotor, 2 carbon brushings"

Part Function
Stator Stationary outer shell with field windings (or permanent magnets) — produces the magnetic field
Rotor Rotating inner element — current through its windings interacts with stator field → torque
Carbon brushes (commutator-type) Sliding contacts that transfer current to the rotating commutator → spin direction depends on which segment is energised
Pivot Pin around which something rotates

Motor types

Notebook (margin): "Large motors are seal motors" (interpreted as: large motors are usually sealed/brushless types)

Modern automotive applications use: - Brushed DC (older starters, wiper motors, window motors) — simple, cheap, brushes wear. - Brushless DC (newer comfort actuators, EPS motors) — electronic commutation, long life. - Series-wound (starter motors) — high starting torque. - Stepper (HVAC flap actuators) — precise positioning.

Diagnostic margin notes (IMG 113404 bottom)

Notebook: "Rectifier faulty: load down [voltage low] & OK [current OK] Cryogenics: Study of materials at low temp Car not starting: Crank position sensor teeth broken If one is/both shoes of drum brake are leading shoe then disadvantage is while reverse both shoes become trailing."

Scattered diagnostic snippets: - Alternator rectifier diodes open-circuit → reduced voltage output → battery discharges, dash voltage indicator drops. - Crank position sensor with damaged/broken reluctor teeth → engine won't start (DME can't time injection/spark). - Drum brake shoes: cross-references to the drum brake page — leading-shoe self-energising effect reverses when wheel direction reverses.

Sensor categories (7)

Notebook (IMG 113407): "Sensor: ① Position sensor — Potentiometer ② Rotational sensor — Crankshaft sensor ③ Temp sensor — Ni wire, −60 °C to 80 °C ④ Pressure sensors — Piezo-crystal ⑤ Acceleration ⑥ Knock sensor — Knock is because of untimely explosion of stoichiometric mixture ⑦ Flow rate — Coil heated → as air flows oil [coil] get cooled → so current is given to maintain that temp → hot film air mass flow meter ⑧ Chemical substances"

Margin annotation: "Pressure & temp both indicate density of air. Not individual."

# Type Element Application
1 Position Potentiometer (variable resistor) Throttle position, pedal position
2 Rotational (speed) Hall or VR (variable reluctance) sensor reading a toothed wheel Crank position, cam position, wheel speed (ABS)
3 Temperature Ni-wire (RTD) or NTC/PTC thermistor Coolant temp, intake air temp, oil temp
4 Pressure Piezo-crystal MAP sensor, oil pressure, fuel rail pressure
5 Acceleration Capacitive MEMS or piezo Airbag triggering, DSC stability sensor
6 Knock Piezo accelerometer on engine block Detects untimely combustion → DME retards ignition
7 Flow rate Hot-film anemometer HFM intake-air-mass sensor
8 Chemical Zirconia (O₂) or NOx-sensing electrode Lambda probe, NOx sensor

Knock sensor principle

Notebook: "Knock is because of untimely explosion of stoichiometric mixture."

Pre-ignition / detonation (often called "pinking" or "knock") happens when the air-fuel mixture self-ignites before the spark plug fires — the resulting pressure spike rings the engine block at ~ 6 kHz. A piezo-electric accelerometer bolted to the block detects this vibration → DME retards ignition timing immediately to suppress further knock.

Air-density sensing — why pressure and temp together

Notebook (margin): "Pressure & temp both indicate density of air. Not individual."

Ideal gas law: $\rho = P / (R T)$ — air density depends on both pressure and temperature. The DME reads MAP (manifold absolute pressure) + IAT (intake air temperature) and computes the actual mass of air in the cylinder for accurate fuel injection. Neither sensor alone is enough.

Key Facts

  • Electric motor = stator + rotor + (in brushed motors) carbon brushes.
  • 7 sensor categories: position, rotational, temperature, pressure, acceleration, knock, flow, chemical (chemical is the 8th written but trainer numbered 7).
  • Temperature sensors split into PTC (R↑ with T) and NTC (R↓ with T) — see dedicated chapter.
  • Knock sensor detects pre-ignition (~6 kHz block ringing) via piezo accelerometer.
  • Pressure + temperature together = air density (neither alone is sufficient for fuel calculation).
  • Diagnostic margin: alternator rectifier failure → low voltage; crank-sensor tooth damage → no-start.

Lead-Acid Battery вҖ” Chemistry & SLI Fundamentals

Source images: IMG20250514112543, IMG20250514112810

Note: Both source pages were originally in Engine_systems/ root and moved to Electronics_electrical/Battery/ during Phase 1.

Overview

A lead-acid battery stores electrical energy as chemical energy in a reversible leadвҖ“lead-dioxideвҖ“sulfuric-acid reaction. The car's main battery is the SLI type вҖ” Start-Lighting-Ignition вҖ” sized to deliver a brief high-current burst for starter cranking, then ride out occasional load with the alternator handling most everything else when running. The page covers the discharge and charge equations, the physical construction of the cells (6 cells Г— 2.1 V = 12.6 V), the electrolyte composition (35% sulfuric acid + 65% water), the Pb-alloy plate design, and the SLI vs deep-cycle distinction with the trainer's "100 m race vs marathon" analogy.

Working Principle

Discharge reaction

Notebook (IMG 112543): "2Pb + 2HвӮӮSOвӮ„ вҶ’ 2PbSOвӮ„ + 4HвӮӮO (simplified, see below) Pb + PbOвӮӮ вҮҢ Pb + 2HвӮӮSOвӮ„ вҮҢ 2PbSOвӮ„ + HвӮӮO"

The actual full discharge reaction (correctly balanced):

$$\text{Pb} + \text{PbO}_2 + 2\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4 \;\;\xrightarrow{\text{discharge}}\;\; 2\text{PbSO}_4 + 2\text{H}_2\text{O}$$

What's happening: - Negative plate (porous Pb) gives up electrons вҶ’ becomes PbSOвӮ„. - Positive plate (PbOвӮӮ) accepts electrons вҶ’ becomes PbSOвӮ„. - HвӮӮSOвӮ„ in the electrolyte combines with the lead to form PbSOвӮ„ (which precipitates on both plates) and HвӮӮO (diluting the acid).

Notebook: "HвӮӮSOвӮ„ turns into HвӮӮO when battery is discharging, so at the same time HвӮӮSOвӮ„ gets dilute because of HвӮӮO."

This is why a discharged battery's specific gravity drops (from ~1.28 fully charged to ~1.13 fully discharged) вҖ” the electrolyte is now mostly water.

Charge reaction (reverse direction)

Notebook: "Now charging: PbSOвӮ„ вҶ’ Pb PbSOвӮ„ вҶ’ PbOвӮӮ HвӮӮO вҶ’ HвӮӮSOвӮ„"

Electricity from the alternator pushes the reaction backward:

$$2\text{PbSO}_4 + 2\text{H}_2\text{O} \;\;\xrightarrow{\text{charge}}\;\; \text{Pb} + \text{PbO}_2 + 2\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4$$

The lead-sulfate on both plates breaks down вҶ’ Pb returns to the negative plate, PbOвӮӮ returns to the positive plate, water gives up its oxygen to reform sulfuric acid вҶ’ electrolyte returns to ~1.28 SG.

The full reversible equation

Notebook box: "Pb + PbOвӮӮ + HвӮӮSOвӮ„ вҮҢ HвӮӮO + PbSOвӮ„ (charging вҶҗвҶ’ discharging)"

Both directions:

$$\text{Pb} + \text{PbO}_2 + 2\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4 \;\;\rightleftharpoons\;\; 2\text{PbSO}_4 + 2\text{H}_2\text{O}$$

Plate construction

Notebook (IMG 112810): "Alloy of lead with Cd, Sb [Antimony]. Shallow/large surface area of battery provides more charge. Truck has bigger battery while bikes have smaller."

The plates are not pure lead вҖ” they're lead alloyed with antimony (Sb) and/or cadmium (Cd): - Pure lead is too soft; alloys add mechanical strength. - Antimony historically (1-3%); gives strength but increases gassing (water loss). - Calcium (modern alternative, not in notes) replaces antimony in maintenance-free batteries.

Plate surface area directly determines current capacity: - Larger / more plates вҶ’ more surface вҶ’ more amps available. - Truck batteries are physically bigger (more plates) than bike batteries. - Plate thickness affects deep-cycle durability (thicker = survives more cycles but slower charge/discharge).

SLI vs deep-cycle вҖ” the "race vs marathon" analogy

Notebook (IMG 112810): "[100 m race]: They provide high current for short time. Deep cycle: вҖ“ Provide continuously provide power for long periods of time вҖ“ Store energy from small wind turbine вҖ“ Thicker plate to have greater capacity."

Two opposing design goals:

SLI (100 m race) Deep cycle (marathon)
Purpose Brief high-current burst (starter cranking) Steady moderate current for hours
Plates Thin, many вҶ’ high surface area вҶ’ high current Thick, fewer вҶ’ robust вҶ’ many discharge cycles
Discharge depth Shallow (5вҖ“10% per crank) Deep (50вҖ“80% per cycle)
Cycle life Designed for ~ 10,000 shallow cycles Designed for ~ 500вҖ“1500 deep cycles
Typical use Cars, motorcycles RV house batteries, off-grid solar/wind storage, golf carts
Cranking amps Very high Low

A car battery is SLI вҖ” it dies fast if used deep-cycle (e.g., leaving lights on overnight repeatedly). Conversely, a deep-cycle battery doesn't have enough cranking current for a cold start.

Why "below 12 V the battery is discharged"

Notebook bullet: "Below 12V battery discharge."

A healthy 12.6 V battery dropping below 12.0 V means: - > 80% of capacity is depleted (specific gravity has fallen to ~ 1.13). - The cranking current available will be marginal. - Continuing to draw below 11 V risks deep discharge which precipitates hard sulfation on the plates (irreversible PbSOвӮ„ crystal growth) вҶ’ permanent capacity loss.

Why 50 mA quiescent limit

Notebook: "50 mA current is allowed to use."

Cross-reference to the energy management system chapter: after key-off and all ECUs asleep, total parasitic draw should be < 50 mA (some specs cite < 80 mA for newer cars). At 50 mA, a healthy 70 Ah battery survives ~ 60 days parked.

Detailed Component Notes

Why 6 cells Г— 2.1 V = 12.6 V

The chemistry of lead-acid produces ~ 2.1 V at the open-circuit terminals of one cell at rest. Six cells in series give the standard automotive 12 V (nominal) / 12.6 V (rested fully charged). For trucks and some heavy equipment, 12 cells Г— 2 = 24 V systems are common.

Why 35% HвӮӮSOвӮ„ and not pure acid

  • Pure HвӮӮSOвӮ„: would freeze around 10 В°C, too viscous, attacks lead plates too aggressively.
  • 35% / 65% mix: balances conductivity (peaks around 35-40% concentration) vs viscosity vs freezing point.
  • Specific gravity (SG) at 35% acid: вүҲ 1.28 (fully charged). Drops with discharge as water is produced.
  • A hydrometer measures SG directly вҶ’ tells you the state of charge.

The "buffer / flashlight" caveat

Notebook: "Buffer, flashlight reduce battery life. As it consume current from alternator."

Cabin flashlight, dome light, glove-box light, etc. drawing from the battery when the engine isn't running cuts into available cranking capacity. The trainer's broader point: only allow the alternator to charge or essential loads to discharge the battery during operation. Leaving lights on at idle puts the alternator in a never-quite-keeping-up battle.

Modern alternative chemistries (not in notebook)

The notebook is from the flooded-cell (FLA) era. Modern variants: - AGM (Absorbent Glass Mat) вҖ” electrolyte absorbed in glass-fibre mats; sealed, deep-cycle capable. - Gel вҖ” electrolyte gelled with silica; deep-cycle. - EFB (Enhanced Flooded Battery) вҖ” improved flooded for start-stop applications. - Lithium-iron-phosphate (LiFePOвӮ„) вҖ” racing, motorcycle, some BMW M cars.

All current BMWs since the E90/E60 era use AGM (sealed, maintenance-free, more tolerant of deep discharge from accessories).

Key Facts

  • Lead-acid chemistry: Pb + PbOвӮӮ + 2HвӮӮSOвӮ„ вҮҢ 2PbSOвӮ„ + 2HвӮӮO (reversible).
  • During discharge: HвӮӮSOвӮ„ converts to HвӮӮO вҶ’ electrolyte dilutes.
  • During charge: HвӮӮO reforms HвӮӮSOвӮ„ вҶ’ electrolyte re-concentrates.
  • SLI battery = Start-Lighting-Ignition; the standard car battery.
  • 12.6 V total, 6 cells Г— 2.1 V each.
  • Electrolyte: 35% sulfuric acid + 65% water.
  • Plate alloy = Pb + Sb (or Pb + Ca on maintenance-free) for strength.
  • Larger plate area = more current capacity (truck > car > bike).
  • SLI vs Deep cycle: thin/many plates (high current, shallow discharge) vs thick plates (deep discharge, many cycles).
  • Below 12 V = discharged; risk of hard sulfation below ~ 11.8 V.
  • 50 mA max permissible quiescent draw (key-off).

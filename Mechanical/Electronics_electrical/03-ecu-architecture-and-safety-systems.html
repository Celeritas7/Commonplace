ECU Architecture & Vehicle Safety Systems

Source images: IMG20250515002244, IMG20250515002251

Notebook date: 26/7/2013

Overview

The ECU (Electronic Control Unit) is the universal control architecture on a modern car: many specific sensors flow into one decision-making computer, which then drives specific actuators. The page gives the 12-sensor input list and 4-actuator output list that a typical engine ECU manages. The companion page then organises the car's full safety system into Active safety (20 things that prevent accidents) and Passive safety (7 things that reduce impact during an accident).

Working Principle

ECU sensor inputs (12)

Notebook (IMG 002244): "Sensors: ① Hot Film air mass sensor (HFM) ② Air temperature sensor ③ Wheel speed sensor ④ Crankshaft position sensor ⑤ Camshaft position sensor ⑥ Fuel level indicator sensor ⑦ Oil coolant sensor ⑧ Knock sensor ⑨ Throttle valve position sensor ⑩ Oxygen sensor ⑪ Tyre pressure sensor ⑫ Rain & light sensor ⑬ [blank]"

The list spans the whole vehicle (not just engine) — typical of a modern car's central or networked ECU view. The blank ⑬ suggests the list was open-ended.

ECU actuator outputs (4)

Notebook (IMG 002244, right column): "Actuators: ① Motors ② Solenoid valves ③ Relays ④ Fuel injector"

The four classes of actuator the ECU commands:

Class Examples
Motors Throttle motor, valvetronic motor, EPS motor, HVAC blower, wiper motors
Solenoid valves VANOS solenoid, EGR valve, purge valve, ABS modulator solenoids
Relays Fuel pump relay, AC clutch relay, starter relay
Fuel injectors Solenoid-actuated needle valves (called out separately because they're so common)

Safety systems (page IMG 002251)

The page splits all safety into two classes — Active and Passive — that align with the vehicle systems hierarchy chapter.

Active safety — helps PREVENT accidents (20 items)

Notebook: "① Driver ② Brake ③ Steering ④ Light ⑤ Wipers ⑥ Heat [defrost] ⑦ AC ⑧ Mirror ⑨ Horn ⑩ Tyres ⑪ ABS ⑫ Adaptive headlight ⑬ Suspensions ⑭ Dynamic control ⑮ RLS [Rain & Light Sensor] ⑯ Cruise control ⑰ Headup display ⑱ Blindspot display ⑲ Reverse system ⑳ Lane assist"

The most striking item: "Driver" listed first — the trainer's reminder that the human is the primary active-safety system. Then mechanical (brakes, steering, tyres), perceptual (lights, wipers, mirrors, horn), electronic active (ABS, DSC, adaptive headlights, lane assist), and information (HUD, blindspot, reverse camera).

Passive safety — REDUCES impact during accident (7 items)

Notebook: "① Air bag [SRS = supplementary restraint system] ② Chassis ③ Windshield ④ Seat belt ⑤ Bumpers ⑥ Crumple zone ⑦ Telescopic steering"

Each item is a passive-mitigation feature:

Passive feature What it does in a crash
Airbag (SRS) Cushions occupant against steering wheel / dashboard / door / roof
Chassis Rigid passenger cell — preserves occupant survival space
Windshield Laminated glass holds together when broken; helps support airbag deployment
Seat belt Restrains occupant from being thrown into car interior
Bumpers Absorb low-speed impacts; isolate body from minor knocks
Crumple zones Deformable structures (front/rear) that absorb energy progressively → slow the deceleration → reduce force on occupants
Telescopic steering Steering column collapses on impact instead of spearing the driver

Active vs Passive timeline at a crash

   t < 0      t = 0           t > 0
   ──────    ───────────     ─────────────
   ACTIVE      ╔CRASH╗       PASSIVE
   ──────      ╚═════╝       ─────────────
   - ABS                     - Crumple zone absorbs energy
   - DSC                     - Bumpers compress
   - lane assist             - Belt pretensioner fires
   - braking                 - Airbag deploys (~30 ms)
   - steering                - Chassis preserves cabin
                             - Telescopic steering collapses

   ► All ACTIVE measures try to avoid t=0 (the crash event).
   ► All PASSIVE measures try to minimise damage AT t=0+.

Key Facts

  • ECU = sensors → logic → actuators (universal pattern).
  • 12 typical sensor inputs to a modern engine ECU: HFM, air temp, wheel speed, crank, cam, fuel level, oil temp, knock, throttle, O₂, tyre pressure, rain/light.
  • 4 actuator classes: motors, solenoid valves, relays, fuel injectors.
  • Active safety = PREVENT accidents. 20 items, headed by "Driver."
  • Passive safety = REDUCE impact during accidents. 7 items: airbag, chassis, windshield, belt, bumpers, crumple zone, telescopic steering.
  • Modern cars have both layered together (active prevents many crashes; passive minimises the consequence when active fails).
